Ben Walker Portfolio 2020

Interactive Sketches

A collection of early drafts for exhibits that I did while at RLMG. These specific exhibits were for Yale Peabody Museum of Natural History, one of the oldest and most prolific natural history museums in the world.

1. Scientific Instruments

Scientific instruments is an interactive exhibit where users get to learn more about the physical objects displayed in the museum. Users pick an object from the home screen, and then swipe along the corresponding timeline to learn more about its use and function in a historical context. They can then click on images and other media along the timeline for a deeper dive.

2. Beyond The Eye

Beyond The Eye is an interactive gallery where users can freely explore various significant optical instruments. Users can move freely through the gallery using common digital gestures like swiping to move, and pinching to zoom. Other UI elements include filtering options, and accessible navigation options.

3. Minerals In Everyday Objects

In Minerals In Everyday Objects, users can explore common minerals that surround them in their daily lives. The mocked up illustration takes place in Connecticut to ground the exhibit in relevance to the user, while the selected objects are things such as bicycles, buildings, sidewalks, cellphones, etc. Users begin by dragging and dropping a mineral into the tray, where corresponding objects glow on the map, showing where that mineral lives.
